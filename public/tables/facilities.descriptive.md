# Facilities (Descriptive)

Facilities are the economic, industrial and scientific "activity" units of each player at each body. Players are described in `players.descriptive.md`. "Body" is any freely orbiting object, such as a planet, moon, asteroid, or spacecraft.

Notes:

1. For national polities on Earth, each facility represents the full territory and all economic, industrial, and scientific activity of that polity.
2. For agencies on Earth, each facility represents all of the agency's ground-based operations.
3. Spacecraft facilities represent a player's segment or contribution aboard a specific station.

- **ISS NASA** — The US Operational Segment, including laboratory modules and the main solar array truss structure that provides the station's primary electrical power.
- **ISS Roscosmos** — The Russian Orbital Segment, including the Zarya and Zvezda service modules with their own supplementary solar panels.
- **ISS ESA** — The European contribution, centered on the Columbus laboratory module. Draws power from the station's shared systems.
- **ISS JAXA** — Japan's contribution, centered on the Kibo laboratory and its exposed experiment facility. Draws power from the station's shared systems.
- **Tiangong CNSA** — China's space station. Note: China's modular space station, fully operational since 2022. The reference body is Tiangong Space Station.
