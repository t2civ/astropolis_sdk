All compositions below are elemental weight percent, summing to 100. Solid resource references represent beneficiated concentrate grade. When used for body/layer composition, each resource's mass fraction refers to its concentrate-equivalent content; rejected gangue is accounted for in the stone or regolith fraction.

## Usage Notes

The stone and regolith references reflect two ends of silicate differentiation: stone is the Mg-rich ultramafic composition that dominates planetary mantles by mass, while regolith is the Ca-Al-Fe-enriched basaltic composition typical of crustal surfaces. Both compositions are body- and layer-specific in practice; these references are solar-system-weighted defaults. Earth's continental crust (granitic, ~72% SiO₂) differs substantially from either reference and would need a per-layer override.

Chemically bound water — as in C-type asteroid phyllosilicates at 10–20 wt% — should be assigned to the water resource category even though extraction requires thermal processing of what is physically a silicate rock. The energy cost and technology of extraction are separate parameters from the resource classification.

Precious metals at ~400 ppm total constitute <0.05% of their ore's mass but dominate its value. The simulation will need to handle value-per-tonne spanning roughly six orders of magnitude across the 27 resource types (from stone at ~\$0/t to precious metal ores valued for their trace PGM content at effectively ~\$10⁵–10⁶/t of contained metal).
